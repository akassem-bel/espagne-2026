The shape of the trip

Three legs, from busiest to calmest. Barcelona first, for the big sights while you're fresh (no car, the city is walkable). Then the drive to Madrid with a lunch stop in Zaragoza, four nights in the capital around the match and a day trip. Finally Valencia: beach, pool, aquarium, no alarm clocks, before heading back to Barcelona for the flight home.

Day trip from Madrid

Chosen: Segovia, the best for a 9- and 12-year-old (fairy-tale castle and Roman aqueduct). The other three stay good options if you change your mind on the day.

Chosen

Segovia

~1 h drive

A 2,000-year-old Roman aqueduct you can walk right up to, and the Alcázar, the castle said to have inspired Disney's. Roast lamb for lunch. The most exciting for kids.

Toledo

~45 min drive

The closest and most dramatic: a whole medieval city on a hill ringed by a river, with a huge cathedral, sword and armour shops, and marzipan. Denser and steeper to walk.

El Escorial

~50 min drive

A colossal royal monastery-palace in the cooler hills northwest of Madrid. Grand and rich in history; the calmer, shadier option on a scorching day. The least fun of the four for kids.

Ávila

~1 h 15 drive

The most complete medieval walls in Europe: you can walk the whole way around on the ramparts. Great for kids who like to climb and explore. A bit farther out.

All are doable by car (you'll have it) or by fast train from Madrid if you'd rather not drive on a day off.
